About Detail Forms

Detail Forms contain information about records, organized by category pages. You can add, edit, and review record information in more detail than in many list views. All Detail Forms contain an Information page that contains the basic information about the record and a toolbar that provides access to common commands.

Detail Forms also contain a Previous Record button and a Next Record button next to the Projects drop-down list so you can navigate to the previous or next record without having to exit the Detail Form.

Project InVision automatically creates links to Detail Form records whenever you complete fields that are links to other items. Field labels underlined in blue font in the Detail Form indicate a hyperlink.

Saving Your Information

Detail Forms contain both a Save button (on the toolbar) and a Submit button. You can make changes on multiple pages, then click Save, and all the changed information on all pages will be saved. Submit saves the same way, but also closes the Detail Form.

The Cancel button reverses any changes you've made only since you last clicked Save.

Required Fields

Most Detail Forms have at least one field on at least one page that is required. Your Workgroup Administrator can define any field to be required, and a field can become required at a particular status in a Workflow. All required fields have labels in bold text.

Calculated Fields

Calculated Fields are fields that show the results of calculations. They can be added to Detail Form pages by the Workgroup Administrator and are not editable. When you change information in fields that affect the calculations and click Refresh or Save, the Calculated Fields are updated.

Non-editable Fields

In addition to Calculated Fields, other fields may appear non-editable. This may happen if:

  • You have only View rights in the module.
  • The fields have been defined as read-only by the Workgroup Administrator.
  • The fields are non-editable at the current Status in the Workflow.
