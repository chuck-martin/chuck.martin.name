Registering Your Company

Company registration involves these tasks:

  • Selecting a service plan
  • Registering your company
  • Registering your System Administrator
  • Entering your billing information
  • Accepting the Terms of Service

As you complete each step in the registration process, click Next to proceed to the next step. Fields with a red asterisk (*) are required fields. If you do not complete these fields, you will receive an error message when you try to proceed to the next page.

Before You Begin

To complete the company registration process, you will need the following information:

  • Company name
  • Company address
  • Company ID (Choose an ID between 3 and 16 characters, starting with a letter. Do not use spaces.)
  • Credit card information

Selecting a Service Plan

Select a subscription package from the available options.

Registering Your Company

Enter the information about your company and then enter a company ID.

You and your employees must enter the Company ID on the Sign-In page whenever you sign in.

Registering Your System Administrator

The System Administrator can access the entire site and controls other users' access to the site. Administrator registration allows you to register yourself as the Administrator or assign someone else to that status.

When you have completed the Administrator Registration step, be sure to read the Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and Security Statement. You must click Accept at the end of the Terms of Service to be able to use the system.

Entering Your Billing Information

Enter the information for the credit card that will be used for payment.

Note: This applies only if you've chosen a service plan that requires payment.

Accepting the Terms of Service (TOS)

Read the Terms of Service (TOS), then click Accept to complete the company registration process. After you accept the Terms of Service, you will receive a welcome e-mail message verifying your company registration.

Initial System Setup

When you click Accept on the Terms of Service page, you will then see the initial System Setup page. From here, you can choose how you want to set up the rest of your company, including entering employee information and giving them access rights, setting up or importing lists of accounts and customers, and more. From here, you can:

  • Outsource the remaining setup, where a third party will take the information you provide and perform all the data input.
  • Delegate the remaining setup to some of your other employees.
  • Perform the remaining setup yourself.

Note: You do not have to set up the rest of your company now; you can perform this setup at any future time.
