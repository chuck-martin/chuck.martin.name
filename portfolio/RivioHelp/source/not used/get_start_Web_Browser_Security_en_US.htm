Web Browser Security

Browser Security Alerts

Web browsers display Security Alerts to notify you about certain situations regarding your computer's security. Because this site uses SSL technology to keep your personal and financial profile secure, you might encounter browser Security Alerts while using the applications. The two most common alerts you might encounter deal with:

  • Secure connections (SSL)
  • Cookies

While using the site, it is normal to enter and leave secure connections or for cookies to be written to your computer, and you should choose to continue when you encounter these alerts. However, you can also choose to hide these Security Alerts to reduce or eliminate any inconvenience they might cause you. For more information, see How to turn off Security Alerts below.

SSL — Secure Connections 

Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) is the industry standard in Web security. It is used primarily for transmitting sensitive information over the Internet. When you have a secure connection between your browser and a Web site, no one else can easily access the data that you send across the connection. This site uses SSL to encrypt the personal and financial data you transmit to us.

Occasionally, your browser might display a security alert to notify you that you are entering or leaving a secure connection. These alerts serve to tell you when the system is taking precautions to keep the information you supply (such as Social Security Number or your payroll information) secure while it travels from your computer to the site server or vice versa. This is normal, and you can choose to continue in both cases.

You can choose not to have your browser display these security alerts. For more information, see How to turn off Security Alerts below.

What is a cookie and how safe is it? 

A cookie is a very small text file that a Web site can save to your computer's hard disk to store information about the site. We do not use cookies to store your profile, your password, address, or any financial information. However, we do use cookies to store the intermediate "state" of the application to provide a richer, more responsive experience to you as a user.

A cookie cannot be run like a program, nor can it deliver viruses. The cookies we place are uniquely yours, and can only be read by the Web site that saved it to your computer.

Occasionally, your browser might display a Security Alert to notify you that a participating partner site is trying to write a cookie to your computer. This is normal, and you should accept the cookie.. For more information about our use of cookies, please review the Privacy Policy for this site.

How to see the cookies saved on your hard drive 

Each brand of browser saves cookies in its own way. Here is how to see the cookies saved by the two most popular browsers. For additional information, refer to your browser’s help system.

Internet Explorer 4.x, 5.x, and 6

  1. Click Tools, and then click Internet Options.
  2. In the General Tab under the Temporary Internet Files section, click the Settings button.
  3. In the Settings dialog box click the View Files button. A list of all cookies on your hard drive appears.
  4. To see details for a cookie, double-click to open it.

Netscape Navigator 6.x

  1. Click Tasks, then Privacy and Security.
  2. Select Cookie Manager from the sub-menu.
  3. Choose View Stored Cookies from the submenu. A list of cookies listed on your computer appears.

Netscape Navigator 4.x, and 6.x

Assuming you have installed the Netscape program in the folder C:\Program Files\Netscape, all the cookies are stored in the text file C:\Program Files\Netscape\Users\default\cookies.txt.

Note: Although you have the ability to modify your browser to either accept all cookies, notify you when a cookie is sent, or reject all cookies, it is not possible to use our services if you reject cookies

How to turn off Security Alerts 

Using Internet Explorer, when either a secure connection alert box or a cookie alert box appears, the box includes a check-box labeled In the future, do not show this warning. Check this box to stop the warning from popping up in the future.

For additional information, refer to your browser’s help system.

How to turn off Secure Connection Alerts 

Internet Explorer 5.x, 6.x

  1. Click Tools, then click Internet Options.
  2. Click the Advanced tab and scroll to the Security section.
  3. In the Security section, clear the Warn if changing between secure and not secure mode check box.

Internet Explorer 4.x

  1. Click View, then click Internet Options.
  2. Click the Advanced tab and scroll to the Security section.
  3. In the Security section, clear the Warn if changing between secure and not secure mode check box.

Netscape Navigator 4.x

  1. Click the Security icon in the top bar; it will pop up another browser window.
  2. Click the Navigator menu item in the left frame.
  3. Clear all four checkboxes displayed under the heading, "Show a warning before".

Netscape Navigator 6.x

1. Click the Security icon in the top bar; it will pop up another browser window.

2. Click the Navigator menu item in the left frame.

3. Clear all four checkboxes displayed under the heading, "Show a warning before".

  1. Click Tasks, then Privacy and Security.
  2. Select Security Manager from the sub-menu.
  3. Click the Applications tab to open it.
  4. Clear all four checkboxes displayed under Set Personal Security Manager to show a warning and ask for permission before:.

How to turn off Cookie Alerts 

Internet Explorer 5.x, and 6

  1. Click Tools, and then click Internet Options.
  2. Click the Security tab.
  3. Click the Internet zone icon.
  4. Select a security level other than "High".
    -or-
    Click Custom Level, scroll to the Cookies section, and then click Enable for both cookie options.

Internet Explorer 4.x

  1. Click View, then click Internet Options.
  2. Click the Advanced tab and scroll to the Security section.
  3. Under Cookies, click Always accept cookies.

Netscape Navigator 6.x

  1. Click Edit, then click Preferences.
  2. Click the Privacy and Security menu on the left side.
  3. Clear the Warn me before accepting the cookie checkbox and choose Enable all cookies.

Netscape Navigator 4.x

  1. Click Edit, then click Preferences.
  2. Click the Advanced menu on the left side.
  3. Clear the Warn me before accepting the cookie checkbox and select Accept all cookies.
